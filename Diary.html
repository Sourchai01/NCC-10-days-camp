NCC Camp Diary

Day - 1 Today’s day started at 3:45 a.m. It felt more special than the previous days—maybe because it was Saturday, maybe because of the food we were expecting, or perhaps because of the possibility of special punishment. Our camp had started on Wednesday, 7 July 2025. Normally, our day begins with a health run, but today it was cancelled. The official reason was the rain, but it almost felt as if nature also wanted to make this day special. After a short break, the real speciality of the day began with breakfast. We were served aloo ki sabji, puri, salad, banana, and special tea. Honestly, what more could one need to survive the day? After breakfast, we attended a lecture by respected L.K. Pandey sir about range firing. Once the lecture finished, we were supposed to get a break of one and a half hours, but instead we were given pineapple squash with chocolate biscuits. Since the day already felt special, we enjoyed and relaxed for another half hour. Later, we had another lecture by L.K. Pandey sir, where he explained more about firing, its positions, and other important details. Then came the most interesting moment of the day—lunch time. The food was amazing. We had unlimited chole bhature, fried rice, ice cream, semai, salad, papad, and achar. These were the highlights of the meal for me. After lunch, we again got a short break of more than one and a half hours. During this time, we usually enjoyed taking a bath and having a little fun and masti with friends. By the time this break ended, it was already 4:00 p.m., which meant it was time to go to the ground for P.T. We started by warming up and running four rounds of the ground, followed by several P.T. exercises. In the sports session, we had many indoor and outdoor games. I especially enjoyed volleyball, where I liked playing as a middle blocker (or middle hitter). I also played chess, which I like to call “the game of the mind.” After spending a good and energetic time playing, we went for dinner. The food was again amazing—paneer, dal, rice, salad, papad, chapati, and even puri. After a short break, we went for roll call, where we had to sit cross-legged on the ground for about one and a half hours. But since it was already a special day, something else also happened after roll call—ragda. Because of some foolish mistakes made by a few cadets, everyone had to face it. There was a lot of shouting and discipline being enforced, and we also received a long lecture of nearly 45 minutes. I wanted to write my diary at night, but the sir kept coming again and again, so I could not write properly at that time. Because of all this, we finally went to sleep at around 11:45 p.m.

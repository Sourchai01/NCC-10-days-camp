10 Days NCC Camp Experience

Day – 4

Today’s day started at 3:45 a.m. It felt more special than the previous days—maybe because it was Saturday, maybe because of the food we were expecting, or perhaps because of the possibility of special punishment. Our camp had started on Wednesday, 7 July 2025. Normally, our day begins with a health run, but today it was cancelled due to rain. It almost felt as if nature also wanted to make this day special. After a short break, the real speciality of the day began with breakfast. We were served aloo ki sabji, puri, salad, banana, and special tea. After breakfast, we attended a lecture by respected L.K. Pandey sir about range firing. Later we had pineapple squash with chocolate biscuits and some relaxation time. Another lecture by L.K. Pandey sir followed, where he explained firing positions and related details. Then came lunch time. The meal included unlimited chole bhature, fried rice, ice cream, semai, salad, papad, and achar. After lunch we received a long break during which we enjoyed bathing and spending time with friends. Later at 4:00 p.m. we went for P.T. where we warmed up by running four rounds of the ground followed by exercises. During sports time I played volleyball as a middle blocker and also spent some time playing chess. Dinner included paneer, dal, rice, salad, papad, chapati, and puri. After dinner we had roll call where we sat cross-legged for a long time. Due to mistakes by some cadets we also experienced “ragda” and received a lecture of nearly 45 minutes. Finally, after a long and memorable day, we went to sleep around 11:45 p.m.

Day – 5

This day was dated 8 June 2025. I woke up at 4:30 a.m., although I had planned to wake up at 4:00 a.m. As the morning assembly was scheduled at 5:30 a.m., there was very little time to complete the usual routine. Therefore, I quickly brushed my teeth, washed my face and legs, and proceeded to the ground. For about one and a half hours there was no specific activity except attendance. Afterwards we returned to our classrooms which were being used as accommodation. Later we were called for drill practice and by 9:00 a.m. we felt quite tired. Breakfast included poha, jalebi, fried chilli and tea. Following breakfast we attended several lectures. A senior cadet shared his experiences and later we had a tea break with cupcakes. Another lecture focused on disaster management including health awareness, first aid, and safety measures during emergencies. Lunch consisted of vegetable biryani, raita, salad, papad, and achar. Later we attended another lecture explaining the importance and advantages of NCC. In the evening we had tea with lemon-flavoured glucose drink and biscuits before going to the ground for sports. I again played volleyball. Dinner included chapati, dal, rice, salad, raita, papad and achar. After dinner we practiced a skit for the camp competitions during roll call and finally marched back to our rooms and went to sleep peacefully.

Day – 6

We woke up before sunrise at 5:00 a.m. but were slightly late for assembly. There was no water available at first because the tanks had become empty due to excessive usage by cadets from different schools. Fortunately, we had stored some water in buckets earlier. Fresh water supply returned around 8:00 a.m. Breakfast included aloo paratha with chutney and tea. The chutney tasted very good. Later we attended a lecture on leadership followed by sugarcane juice and biscuits. One of the most interesting lectures of the camp was conducted by officers from Bhopal on Cyber Security and Hacking. It was highly informative. Lunch included dum aloo, chapati, rice, masoor dal, salad, papad, puri and ice cream. After lunch we watched cultural performances in the auditorium where many cadets from different schools presented their talents. Later during sports time I played volleyball and our team won two matches. Unfortunately I started feeling body pain and fever afterwards. Medical facilities were available in the camp and I received medicine. Dinner included mixed dal, chapati, rice, salad, papad, achar and raita. After roll call we returned to our classrooms and rested on mattresses placed on the ground.

Day – 7

We woke up at 4:30 a.m. as competitions were scheduled. Due to fever and body pain I could not bathe that morning. After the morning routine we participated in tug of war competition but our team lost quickly. Still it was a learning experience. I visited the medical room and rested for some time. Breakfast included pav bhaji with two types of chutney. Later we attended a lecture by a senior cadet and then had lunch consisting of chole sabji, methi puri, jeera fried rice, salad and papad. In the afternoon we attended a session on air modelling where we learned to build small aircraft models. I successfully built a model plane that was able to fly. Unfortunately my fever returned and I again rested in the medical room for several hours. Dinner included mixed vegetables, dal, roti, rice, kheer and salad. During roll call I interacted with cadets from different schools and made new friends. At around 10:00 p.m. we returned to our rooms and slept.

Day – 8

This day was again unusual as my fever continued. After the early morning routine I attended breakfast which included puri and aloo sabji. After taking medicine I rested for about two hours. Later I watched a drill competition taking place on the ground. We then attended a lecture on army schemes in the auditorium although I could only stay for half the session due to health issues. Lunch included cabbage sabji, mixed dal, rice, roti, salad, papad and achar. Later we practiced for cultural competitions and watched performances by other schools. Dinner at 7:30 p.m. included rajma chawal, jeera rice, salad and papad. In the evening gentle rain began falling and the cool wind created a peaceful atmosphere. Watching the rain from the auditorium became one of the memorable moments of the camp. After roll call we returned to our rooms knowing that the next day would be the final day of the camp.

Day – 9

The last full day of the camp felt very different from the previous days. Everyone woke up early with excitement and reflection. After roll call the drill competition took place where different schools presented their performances. Our school performed reasonably well. Later we took many photographs and enjoyed breakfast consisting of poha, jalebi and tea. The cultural programme was held in the auditorium where competition results were announced. Although our school did not win any prizes, the experience motivated us. The final lecture was delivered by the CEO sir who spoke about the values and importance of NCC. Lunch was one of the best meals of the camp including paneer, methi puri, tuvar dal, fried rice, salad, papad, raita, achar, gulab jamun and ice cream. Throughout the day we continued taking photographs, singing and dancing with friends. Dinner included aloo sabji, urad dal, rice, chapati, salad and papad. The final roll call was emotional as L.K. Pandey sir reflected on the entire camp and reminded us of the spirit of NCC. Later we spent time talking, singing and enjoying the final night before eventually going to sleep.

Day – 10

This day marked the departure from the camp and carried mixed emotions of happiness and sadness. I woke up very early at around 3:30 a.m. while the surroundings were still dark and peaceful. After the brief morning roll call we packed our belongings and prepared to leave. Breakfast included aloo sabji, puri, salad, papad and tea. Each cadet also received a refreshment box containing chips, cupcakes, sweets, burger and toffees. Soon afterwards we said goodbye to the friends and cadets with whom we had spent these unforgettable ten days. Finally our bus arrived and we departed safely for home. These ten days taught us valuable lessons about discipline, teamwork, responsibility and personal growth. The camp truly became a memorable and meaningful experience for every cadet.

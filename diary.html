My 10 Days NCC Camp Experience

Welcome Cadet

Day 4

Today’s day started at 3:45 a.m. It felt more special than the previous days—maybe because it was Saturday, maybe because of the food we were expecting, or perhaps because of the possibility of special punishment. Our camp had started on Wednesday, 7 July 2025. Normally, our day begins with a health run, but today it was cancelled. The official reason was the rain, but it almost felt as if nature also wanted to make this day special. After a short break, the real speciality of the day began with breakfast. We were served aloo ki sabji, puri, salad, banana, and special tea. Honestly, what more could one need to survive the day? After breakfast, we attended a lecture by respected L.K. Pandey sir about range firing. Once the lecture finished, we were supposed to get a break of one and a half hours, but instead we were given pineapple squash with chocolate biscuits. Since the day already felt special, we enjoyed and relaxed for another half hour. Later, we had another lecture by L.K. Pandey sir, where he explained more about firing, its positions, and other important details. Then came the most interesting moment of the day—lunch time. The food was amazing. We had unlimited chole bhature, fried rice, ice cream, semai, salad, papad, and achar. These were the highlights of the meal for me. After lunch, we again got a short break of more than one and a half hours. During this time, we usually enjoyed taking a bath and having a little fun and masti with friends. By the time this break ended, it was already 4:00 p.m., which meant it was time to go to the ground for P.T. We started by warming up and running four rounds of the ground, followed by several P.T. exercises. In the sports session, we had many indoor and outdoor games. I especially enjoyed volleyball, where I liked playing as a middle blocker (or middle hitter). I also played chess, which I like to call “the game of the mind.” After spending a good and energetic time playing, we went for dinner. The food was again amazing—paneer, dal, rice, salad, papad, chapati, and even puri. After a short break, we went for roll call, where we had to sit cross-legged on the ground for about one and a half hours. But since it was already a special day, something else also happened after roll call—ragda. Because of some foolish mistakes made by a few cadets, everyone had to face it. There was a lot of shouting and discipline being enforced, and we also received a long lecture of nearly 45 minutes. I wanted to write my diary at night, but the sir kept coming again and again, so I could not write properly at that time. Because of all this, we finally went to sleep at around 11:45 p.m..

Day 5

This day was dated 8 June 2025. I expected the day to begin at 4:00 a.m., but I woke up at 4:30 a.m.. As we had to assemble at 5:30 a.m. every morning, there was very little time to complete the usual morning routine. Therefore, I quickly brushed my teeth, washed my face and legs, and proceeded to the ground. For nearly one and a half hours, we did not have any specific activity. The officers only took our attendance, after which we returned to our rooms, which were actually classrooms used for accommodation. After a short break in the rooms, we were called for drill practice. By this time, it was already around 9:00 a.m., and we felt quite tired after the long session. Following the drill, we were served breakfast, which included poha, jalebi, and fried chilli, along with tea. However, I did not prefer the tea. After breakfast, the regular daily activities continued. We attended a lecture session in which a senior cadet spoke for about half an hour and shared his experiences. This was followed by a tea break, during which we were served cupcakes and tea. At 11:45 a.m., we attended another lecture. During the first part, we were informed about various competitions to be conducted during the camp. In the second part, the session focused on health, physical and mental well-being, first aid, and safety measures during emergencies such as bomb blasts and other disasters. All these topics were discussed under the subject of Disaster Management. After the lecture, we proceeded for lunch, which is always an enjoyable time of the day. The meal included vegetable biryani, raita, salad, papad, and achar. Although it was a simple meal, it was satisfying. After lunch, we attended another lecture in which we learned about the importance and advantages of NCC. Later in the afternoon, as usual, we had another tea break before sunset, during which we were given lemon-flavoured glucose drink and twenty biscuits. After the evening assembly, we went to the ground for sports activities. During this time, I played volleyball, which is the sport I enjoy the most. Following the sports session, we were given a short break of about one and a half hours. Afterwards, it was time for dinner, which included chapati, dal, rice, salad, raita, achar, and papad. After finishing dinner and washing our plates, we spent a short time interacting with friends. Later, we attended the roll call, during which we also practiced a skit, as it was one of the easier activities to participate in during the camp competitions. Finally, after completing all the scheduled activities, we marched back in line to our rooms and went to sleep peacefully, bringing the day to an end.

Day 6

On this day, we woke up before sunrise at 5:00 a.m.. However, we were slightly late again, as we were required to assemble on the ground at 5:30 a.m. There was no water available at that time because sometimes cadets from other schools used excessive water, causing the tanks to become empty. Anticipating such a situation, we had already stored some water in buckets in our room the previous day to manage our basic morning activities. Therefore, I brushed my teeth and completed the necessary tasks. Fresh water supply became available again at around 8:00 a.m. After that, we had breakfast, which included aloo paratha with chutney and tea. The chutney was particularly good. Following breakfast, we attended a lecture on leadership, which was informative and useful. Later, we had a short break during which we were served sugarcane juice along with biscuits. After this break, we attended one of the most interesting lectures of the camp. The topic was Cyber Security and Hacking, and for this session, the unit had invited officers from Bhopal. The lecture was highly engaging and informative, and I personally found it to be the best lecture compared to the others. It clearly showed that the camp included both productive learning and enjoyable activities. After the lecture, it was time for lunch. The meal included dum aloo, chapati, salad, rice, masoor dal, papad, puri, and ice cream. It was a satisfying meal, and some dishes were new for me. Following a short break, we went to the auditorium to watch the cultural performances presented by cadets from different schools. We observed many talented participants. Although the number of highly talented cadets seemed slightly lower this year, it was still sufficient for participation in the competitions. Among nearly twenty participating schools, our school was also selected for a performance, although it was not scheduled for that day. After another break of about one and a half hours, I took a bath and then went to the ground for sports activities. As usual, I played volleyball, and our team successfully won two matches. However, after the games, I began to feel significant body pain, and I suspected that I might have developed a fever. Fortunately, the camp provided medical facilities, and in serious situations the staff could contact our parents. Cadets were also allowed to call their parents if necessary and request any required items to be sent to the camp. Eventually, it was time for dinner, which is usually the most awaited activity of the day. However, due to my physical discomfort and possible fever, the food did not taste as good as usual. The dinner included mixed dal, chapati, rice, salad, papad, achar, and raita. The final activity of the day was the roll call, during which we assembled, attendance was taken, and information regarding upcoming competitions and daily reports was shared. After that, we returned to our classrooms. During this time, we spent some moments enjoying and interacting with friends, and a few cadets even danced for a short while. Finally, we prepared for rest. As there were no beds available, we slept on mattresses placed on the ground, bringing another day of the camp to an end.

Day 7

On this day, we woke up before dawn at 4:30 a.m., as several competitions were scheduled. I woke up with significant body pain and fever, which had started the previous day, so I was unable to take a bath that morning. After completing the regular morning roll call, health run, and drill, the competitions began. One of the main events was tug of war. Our school team participated, but we could hold the rope for only about ten seconds before being defeated. Although we lost, it was still a valuable experience and we learned from it. After the event, I went to the medical room to take medicine and rested there for about half an hour. Later, at around 8:40 a.m., we had breakfast, which included pav bhaji served with two types of chutney. It was something new that we had not received earlier in the camp. After taking the medicine again, I slept for about two hours. Meanwhile, the tug of war competitions continued, and cadets from our school were still participating. When I felt slightly better, I joined them for some time. Later, we attended a lecture delivered by a senior cadet in the auditorium. After the session, it was time for lunch, which included chole ki sabji, methi puri, jeera fried rice, salad, papad, and achar. The meal was very tasty, and by that time my fever had reduced slightly, so I was finally able to enjoy the food properly. In the afternoon, we gathered in the auditorium on the ground for a session on air modelling. During this activity, we learned about the basics of model aircraft and were also given the opportunity to build small model planes. The instructor informed us that this activity had been introduced for the first time in this camp, and considerable effort and resources had been used to organize it for the cadets from nearly twenty participating schools. I listened carefully, learned the process, and successfully constructed one model plane, which was also able to fly. However, later in the day my fever returned, and I again went to the medical room. This time I not only took medicine but also rested there for about four hours under the cooler. After eating two bananas, I began to feel much better and regained my energy. In the evening, I spent some time walking around the ground until it was time to assemble for dinner. The daily routine was slightly different on this day because the competitions and their preparations were ongoing, so cadets were given more flexibility to move between different areas. For dinner, we were served mixed vegetables, mixed dal, roti, rice, kheer, salad, and achar. After finishing the meal and washing our utensils, we assembled for the roll call. During these gatherings, I continued to interact with cadets from different schools and made new friends. Through these conversations, I also learned several new things about their experiences and interests. At around 10:00 p.m., we returned to our classrooms. After writing about the day’s experiences in my diary, I finally went to sleep along with the other cadets, bringing the day to a close.

Day 8

This day was also different from the usual routine. I felt normal during the morning roll call, but afterwards I realized that I was again suffering from fever. We woke up at 4:30 a.m. As usual, we quickly freshened up. I brushed my teeth and washed my hands and legs. Generally, I preferred to take a bath after lunch, as we normally received a break of about one and a half hours during that time. Due to the fever, I only attended breakfast, which included puri and aloo ki sabji. After taking medicine, I went back to the classroom and rested for about two hours. During that time, I came to know that a drill competition was taking place on the ground. Since drill was not one of my strongest activities, I only went to observe the competition for a short time. After the tea break, we assembled in the auditorium at around 11:30 a.m. for a lecture on army schemes and related information. However, because of my health condition, I was able to attend only half of the lecture. After the lecture, we proceeded for lunch, which included cabbage (gobi) sabji, mixed dal, rice, salad, achar, roti, and papad. Later, we continued practicing for the cultural competitions. One important announcement was that our skit had not been selected, but the JW (Junior Wing) cadets from our school were selected, which meant that our school still had representation in the competitions. We then watched performances from several other schools, which were interesting and enjoyable. After some time, we assembled for dinner at around 7:30 p.m. The meal included rajma chawal, jeera rice, salad, papad, and achar. After dinner, we spent some time enjoying the evening. It had started to rain lightly, and gentle raindrops could be felt through the open windows. The cool wind and the soft rainfall created a pleasant natural atmosphere, making the moment quite memorable. Later, I casually went to the auditorium to see what was happening there. Since the selection process for all schools had not yet been completed, some schools were still presenting their performances. From there, the rainfall could be experienced even more beautifully, and it became a memorable moment of the camp. While speaking with a few cadets, I suddenly noticed that many schools were assembling for the NCC roll call. When I reached there, some cadets informed me that others had been searching for me, as they thought I might have gone missing. After the roll call, we returned to our classrooms and prepared to rest. Most of the cadets were already thinking about the fact that the next day would be the final day of the camp. Soon after, we all went to sleep, concluding another day of the camp experience.

Day 9

The following 24 hours were very different from the previous 192 hours that every cadet had spent in this memorable camp. As it was the last day of the camp, everyone woke up before sunrise with a feeling of excitement and reflection. After completing the usual morning routine, I went to the ground for the morning roll call and attendance. Soon after, the drill competition began, where cadets from different schools presented their drill performances. Our school also participated, and although our performance was average, it was still a valuable experience for all of us. After the competition, we spent some time taking photographs and interacting with fellow cadets, realizing that this day would be filled with memories and photographs. Later, we had breakfast, which included yellow poha, jalebi, and hot tea. After finishing the meal, we washed our plates near the water tanker placed close to the building for cleaning utensils. We then returned to our classroom, where we spent some enjoyable time dancing, singing, and interacting with friends, even within the disciplined environment of the camp. After some time, we went to the auditorium to attend the cultural programme. During the event, the results of the earlier competitions were also announced. Unfortunately, our school did not win any of the competitions. Although we felt a little disappointed, the experience motivated us and we learned valuable lessons from it. Following this, we attended the final lecture of the camp, where the CEO sir addressed the cadets and shared his thoughts about the importance and values of NCC. After the lecture, we proceeded for lunch, which was one of the most elaborate meals of the camp. The menu included paneer, methi puri, tuvar dal, matar fried rice, salad, papad, raita, achar, gulab jamun, and ice cream. After enjoying the meal, we spent time taking photographs, dancing, singing, and interacting with friends, making the most of the remaining moments of the camp. Later, after the tea break, I went to the ground to play volleyball, which had become my favourite activity during the camp. After playing for some time, I returned to the classroom. As mentioned earlier, the day continued to be filled with photography sessions, and this time we also took photographs with the third-year SD cadets. In the evening, we proceeded for dinner, which included aloo ki sabji, urad dal, rice, salad, papad, achar, and chapati. After finishing the meal and cleaning our utensils, we returned to the room and spent some time dancing, talking, and enjoying music. Some senior NCC cadets and army personnel also played songs, and many cadets joined in dancing. After about an hour, we were called for the final roll call by the seniors. During roll call, strict discipline was maintained. If cadets did not arrive on time, the seniors would start counting 5, 4, 3, 2, 1, and anyone who failed to assemble within that time had to face the consequences. As per the daily routine, there were three roll calls each day—the first at 5:30 a.m., the second at 4:30 p.m., and the final one at 8:55 p.m. However, this roll call felt special because it was the last roll call of the camp and also the final gathering with all the cadets. During this roll call, L.K. Pandey sir shared a brief reflection on the entire camp, giving us a meaningful understanding of the spirit and discipline of NCC. After the gathering ended, we spoke with our friends and newly made acquaintances before returning to our rooms. The room felt quite humid, so I decided to take a quick bath using three buckets of water, considering it as one of the final memories of the camp. Later, we spent some more time singing songs ourselves, talking, and walking around the area, while cadets from other schools were also enjoying their last moments together. Eventually, around 11:30 p.m., most cadets prepared to sleep. However, I found it slightly difficult to sleep because of a rumour circulating among the cadets about “Batla,” a supposed horror legend of the camp area. Later, it became clear that it was only a baseless rumour, and eventually everyone fell asleep, bringing the last night of the camp to an end.

Day 10

This day was not only the day of departure, but also a day filled with mixed emotions—joy, satisfaction, and a sense of completion, along with a quiet sadness in the heart. After spending ten memorable days together, every cadet seemed to feel that the journey had finally come to an end. On this day, I woke up very early at around 3:30 a.m. The surroundings were still dark, and the calm atmosphere created a feeling of curiosity and reflection. After completing my basic morning routine, I prepared myself and rested for a short time again. Later, I woke up at around 5:30 a.m., and within a few minutes we assembled for the morning roll call. As the day was already scheduled for departure, the roll call was brief. Attendance was taken quickly, and there was very little time for conversation. After returning to the classroom, we packed our bags and belongings, preparing to leave the camp. Soon after, we proceeded for breakfast, which included aloo ki sabji, puri, salad, papad, and tea. In addition, each cadet was provided with a refreshment box that contained chips, cupcakes, sweets, a burger, and toffees. After breakfast, we spent some time saying goodbye to the friends and cadets with whom we had shared these ten days of experiences and memories. Shortly afterwards, our bus arrived, and we prepared for our return journey. Finally, we departed from the camp and returned home safely. These ten days of the camp taught us many valuable lessons. They showed us the importance of discipline, teamwork, responsibility, and personal growth. The experience also helped us understand how life can be lived in a more organized, productive, and disciplined manner, making the camp truly memorable for every cadet who participated.
